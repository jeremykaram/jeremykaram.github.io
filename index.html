Research Interests

My research interests are in star cluster formation through numerical simulations. I have performed many numerical simulations which couple hydrodynamics with N-body to study star cluster evolution embedded inside giant molecular clouds. I have studies clusters dynamics to compare to Gaia observations.

I have focused on realistic initial conditions in my simulations by performing zoom ins from previously run giant molecular cloud simulations.

Non-Research Interests

I have spent the last three years volunteering at the W.J. McCallion Planetarium at McMaster and have given over one hundred public planetarium shows. I also managed the McMaster University Portable Planetarium outreach initiative where I brought my love of astronomy to underrepresented communities across Ontario.

I began my academic career in Jazz guitar studies and have been playing guitar since I was eleven years old. I am very interested in the statistics associated with backgammon and the computer models used to study them.
