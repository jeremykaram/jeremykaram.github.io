Dynamics of Star Cluster Formation: The Effects of Ongoing Star Formation and Stellar Feedback

Jeremy Karam, Michiko Fujii, Alison Sills Published 2025 to The Astrophysical Journal.
arXiv:2503.21716.

X

Left: Angular momentum of stars bound to cluster after a merger with another cluster. This figure shows that a disproportionate fraction of the total angular momentum comes from stars that were accreted onto the cluster from a nearby filament. Right: Kinematic (rotation velocities, and velocity anisotropy) profiles of a merged cluster after gas expulsion. This figure shows that clusters can leave their embedded phase with dynamics inherited from assembly inside their embedded molecular cloud.

Binary Disruption and Ejected Stars from Hierarchical Star Cluster Assembly

Claude Cournoyer-Cloutier & Jeremy Karam et al. Published 2024 to The Astrophysical Journal.
arXiv:2409.13564.

X

Left: The number of disrupted binary systems as a function of time in a hierarchical star cluster assembly simulation with a background gas distribution (IB1), isolated cluster simulations (PB) and a merger of two idealized clusters without a background gas distribution (PM). This shows that cluster mergers inside embedded gas environments lead to the destrcution of binary star systems. Right: The distribution of unbound stars after a star cluster merger with a realistic background gas distribution. This shows that unbound stars have a preferential direction of motion that is dependant on the geometry of the cluster merger they are produced from.

arXiv:2404.06348.

X
